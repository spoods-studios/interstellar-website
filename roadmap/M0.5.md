# Roadmap Detail — M0.5 Gravity Performance

> **Granular retroactive roadmap.** One entry per execution phase
> (2026-07-21). Master roadmap stays milestone-level. Deep-dives:
> posted in #technical-devlog.

**Milestone:** M0.5 Gravity Performance — a deterministic universal-variable
Kepler solver, a Wisdom-Holman (WH) symplectic base (KDK, democratic-
heliocentric), a predictive periapsis encounter trigger, a massless
test-particle tier, and honest fixed-accuracy benchmarking. Scope research
found the O(N²) direct-force loop is not the small-N
bottleneck — close-encounter handling is — so the perf lever is bigger
regular-regime steps (WH), not fully-general per-pair MERCURIUS switching
(deferred). WH ships built, validated, and **disabled**: the honest
benchmark showed it is a step-size win for large future systems, not a
small-N speed win, so Yoshida4 remains the shipped integrator. Opened
2026-06-14, closed 2026-06-20 (gate passed 2026-06-16).
**Era:** Era 0 (engine foundations).
**Phases:** 8 entries — numbered 23–29 plus inserted 27.5 (global numbering
continues from M0.4's 18–22.1); the review-gate fix series is bundled as
"Phase 29: Findings + Fixes."

## Phase 23: Deterministic Kepler Solver — ✅ 2026-06-14

**Goal:** deliver a standalone, fully-tested deterministic universal-variable
Kepler propagator in float64 — the foundation Phase 24's WH drift step
consumes. Front-loaded: this phase carries the milestone's single biggest
risk (regime-selection / iteration-count nondeterminism).

**What it delivered:**
- `det_stumpff(std::array<double,4>&, z)` — REBOUND `stumpff_cs`-pattern
  Stumpff series `c0..c3`, pure `+−×÷`: power-of-two quartering to `|z|≤1`
  (`kReduceLimit=1.0`, wider than REBOUND's `0.1` to keep the Kepler hot
  path on zero doublings), order-17 Horner over an inverse-factorial table
  to `1/19!`, quarter-angle doubling reconstruction. No `√`, no libm,
  no trig — supersedes the scope-locked `det_sin`/`det_cos`: smaller
  deterministic surface, no Cody-Waite reduction, no `(1−cos√z)/z`
  cancellation at z→0
- `kepler_step(x0,v0,dt,k) → KeplerState` — one branchless path over the
  geometric `β = 2/r0 − v0²/k` (a pinned convention); the Vallado/Stiefel
  χ-formulation replaced an initial literal tiny-X time-equation approach
  that round-tripped only to ~2e-8 — ill-conditioned, not N-limited; a
  two-tier Vallado/Mikkola-1987-cubic seed routed through `det_cbrt`/
  `det_sqrt`, never `std::cbrt`/`std::sqrt` (`det_sqrt` added this phase,
  bit-seed + fixed Heron)
- Corrector swapped Danby quartic → **Laguerre-Conway order n=5** mid-phase
  (2026-06-14): a full-domain sweep empirically falsified the quartic
  (diverges e≥0.9 off-periapsis, e≥0.99 at WH-realistic steps, hyperbolic);
  Laguerre-Conway is globally convergent and clears the whole grid
- `kKeplerIterations` **locked = 8** via a convergence sweep (worst margin
  0.057 at e=0.99 near periapsis) plus a Brouwer √t symplecticity check
  (slope 0.314, N/N+4/N+8 bit-invariant) — fixed count, no early
  exit/tolerance/FP-`==`/bisection
- A frozen 7-case golden-vector `==` table (circular, eccentric,
  tilted-radial, near-parabolic, hyperbolic, small-dt, large-dt) plus a
  root-`CMakeLists.txt` `FATAL_ERROR` guard against `-ffast-math`/`-Ofast`
- Exit gate: 308/308 green (baseline 297 → +11 additive cases);
  `grep -nE 'std::(sin|cos|sqrt|pow|fma|cbrt)'` on `kepler_universal.cpp`
  empty except comments — no libm transcendental on the path

**Key decisions:** Stumpff power series over `det_sin`/`det_cos` (smaller
deterministic surface); Laguerre-Conway over Danby (the quartic is only
locally convergent, empirically falsified by the full-domain sweep);
Vallado/Stiefel χ over the initial literal time equation (its tiny-X root
was ill-conditioned); fixed N set by the Brouwer-slope symplecticity test,
not per-step residual (an under-converged corrector shows biased linear
energy drift a residual check misses); golden-vector `==` as the real
cross-platform determinism guard, the compiler flag necessary but not
sufficient.

**Deviations:** two fixes mid-phase — the χ-formulation swap (a round-trip
failure) and the Danby→Laguerre-Conway corrector swap (a divergence
failure), both user-approved at their checkpoint. A portable-`memcpy` fix
followed for the MSVC CI lane. Post-phase: Phase 24 wires WH's drift step
directly onto `kepler_step`; Phase 29 raised `kKeplerIterations` 8→12 (an
out-of-domain revolutions-per-step gap the original sweep didn't sample)
and consolidated `det_sqrt`/typed the `det_stumpff` buffer extent; Phase 34
made the test seams `thread_local` and narrowed the domain claim to exclude
subnormal `r0`.

**Deep-dive:** posted in #technical-devlog

## Phase 24: Wisdom-Holman Map — ✅ 2026-06-15

**Goal:** replace/augment the REGULAR-tier integrator with a Wisdom-Holman
symplectic map — an exact Kepler drift per body about a dominant central
mass, operator-split against a heliocentric interaction kick — so a
star-dominated table can take a much larger regular-regime step without
secular energy drift; Yoshida4 and the IAS15 close-encounter fallback stay
unchanged.

**What it delivered:**
- `wisdom_holman.hpp`/`physics_integrator_wh.cpp` — democratic-heliocentric
  frame transforms (dominant slot stores absolute position, exact
  single-pass inverse), the heliocentric interaction kick (non-dominant
  bodies only, excludes the dominant attraction the drift integrates
  exactly), the momentum-only jump term folded into the drift, `wh_step`
  KDK composition — all `+,−,×,÷` only, one hand-rolled `det_sqrt` root,
  never `std::sqrt`
- A new locked WH-KICK summation order: dr sign, `G·mass·inv_r3` prefactor
  form, ascending-pair-visit order with the dominant slot skipped —
  locked; any change requires re-verification
- KDK over the plan's DKD wording: a DKD step propagates two-body as
  `kepler_step(dt/2)∘kepler_step(dt/2)`, analytically but not bit-for-bit
  equal to `kepler_step(dt)`; KDK collapses the massless two-body case to
  `kepler_step(dt)` exactly, satisfying the bit-exact split-correctness
  probe. Symplectic correctors (Wisdom 2006) deferred — plain KDK already
  meets the energy-boundedness requirement
- `Wh` added to `IntegratorMethod` + the worker's `method` variant, ctor
  auto-select (star-dominated keeps WH, else downgrades to Yoshida4, never
  throws); ENCOUNTER arm and the WH↔IAS15 handoff byte-unchanged
  (barycentric Cartesian throughout)
- Same-binary byte-identity + time-reversal round-trip (residual scales
  linearly with N, ~N·6.5e-11 — round-off, not secular); Brouwer energy
  bounded/non-secular over ~634 yr (two-body max |ΔE/E| 3.6e-9, multi-body
  3.4e-5); step calibration locks `kDominantMassRatio = 100.0` and a
  stable-step bound of ~3% of the shortest orbital period
- Production seed flipped to `Wh{}` at `step_dt = 21600.0` s (72× the prior
  Yoshida4 step); frozen cross-platform WH golden-vector table (5 cases,
  IEEE-754-bit-reconstructed inputs) asserted bit-exact `==` on both CI
  lanes

**Key decisions:** additive `Wh` tag over a Yoshida4 replacement (bit-
identity locks plus a no-dominant-mass fallback); frame transform confined
to the step boundary so every other seam stays byte-unchanged; KDK over
DKD to satisfy the hard bit-exact split-correctness probe; correctors
deferred (plain KDK already meets the energy-boundedness requirement);
calibrate-then-lock for `kDominantMassRatio` and the stable-step bound.

**Deviations:** the DKD-to-KDK composition change from the plan's literal
wording (justified in-phase, all prior locks hold under KDK). Post-phase
(same day): Phase 27 found the shipped map froze the dominant body (no
barycenter update) causing secular drift — production reverted to
Yoshida4, fixed in Phase 27.5 (COM-anchor transform); Phase 27 closed with
WH not beating Yoshida4 at the shipped config (time-warp is its real win);
M0.6 narrowed WH's shipped scope to the heliocentric-valid Sun/EMB/Mars/
Jupiter config after the Moon's η ≈ 0.40 failed the perturbation gate;
satellites moved to the new M0.7 nested-WH milestone.

## Phase 25: Predictive Periapsis Estimator — ✅ 2026-06-15

**Goal:** replace M0.4's reactive mutual-Hill-radius detector with a
step-boundary predictive trigger — a closed-form two-body periapsis
estimate from step-start relative state, frozen and time-symmetric — so
the switch to IAS15 fires before a fast flyby's periapsis falls between
step samples, and so the switch itself is provably reversible (Hernandez &
Dehnen 2023: naive reactive switching injects secular energy error per
crossing).

**What it delivered:**
- `predicted_periapsis`/`predicted_apoapsis` — closed-form all-conic
  estimator `q = p/(1+e)` from the conserved two-body scalars `(r², v²,
  r·v, μ)`; `r·v` enters only squared (Lagrange identity), so `q` is
  EXACTLY invariant under `v → −v`; both internal roots route through a
  file-local det-form sqrt, no libm on the path. Matched an analytic conic
  oracle to 1e-9 and an independent `kepler_step`-propagated min-|x| oracle
  across near-circular/moderate-e/near-parabolic/hyperbolic states
- `detect_regime_predictive` — a new STATELESS step-boundary predicate (no
  `prior`, no history): reuses the M0.4 dominant-body scan, `hill_radius`,
  and bound-pair exemption verbatim; a `v→−v`-symmetric horizon gate
  (`t* = −(r·v)/v²`) plus a squared switch-IN compare
  `q² < pred_k²·R_Hill²·pred_pad`; `pred_pad` is the sole anti-chatter
  margin; NaN/corrupt/boundary tables fall through to REGULAR
- Worker seam swap (`run_due_steps`): `detect_regime` →
  `detect_regime_predictive`, `regime_` now observe-only; an in-phase fix
  preserved the M0.4 lone-sun-grazer protection a pair-only predicate would
  have dropped (a new stateless dominant-timescale switch-in knob, reused
  verbatim from M0.4)
- Primary bad-switch detector: forward-N/flip-v/back-N/flip-v through a
  genuine REGULAR↔ENCOUNTER crossing returns to start within `N·1e-10`;
  anti-thrash band-edge pair makes ≤4 transitions over 2000 steps; a
  sub-sample straddle negative control (predictive fires, unmodified
  `detect_regime` does not, same state); same-binary byte-identity
  confirmed through the new seam
- Calibrate-then-lock: a `(pred_k, pred_pad, pred_horizon_n)` grid sweep
  pins the knee **(3.0, 1.21, 2.0)** — smallest band that simultaneously
  catches the flyby negative-control case (113× margin), produces zero
  thrash, keeps the bad-switch detector bounded, and leaves the Earth-Moon
  bound pair in REGULAR

**Key decisions:** closed-form conic over a ballistic line or repeated
kepler-step sampling (exact two-body physics, one evaluation, no extra
reversibility surface); stateless pad-only anti-chatter over a
historyful `k_in`/`k_out` band (a history band is exactly the
reversibility hazard the paper names); predicate swap, not seam rewrite,
so WH/IAS15/snapshot/`system_energy` stay untouched; calibrate-then-lock
for the trigger constants.

**Deviations:** none from the locked forks. In-phase fix: a lone-grazer
protection gap surfaced by a worker-lock test was closed the same wave
with a new switch-in knob rather than deferred.

## Phase 26: Massless Test-Particle Tier — ✅ 2026-06-15

**Goal:** turn the M0.4 per-side `gravitating` gate (already feel-but-not-source
physics) into a genuine O(N_active·N) cost tier: bodies above an explicit
`N_active` cut feel gravity from the actives but source none, and test-test
pairs are structurally never computed — not skipped by a branch.

**What it delivered:**
- `compute_accelerations` restructured into two sequential blocks: an active
  block bounded by `n_active` (byte-for-byte the existing kernel, only the
  bound changed) + an appended test block summing gravitating actives only
  (a locked summation order) — no write-back, no test-test inner loop
- A load-bearing lock: active-body accelerations BIT-IDENTICAL (`==`) with
  vs without appended test particles, plus a partition-permutation guard;
  the visit count pinned empirically via a test-local pair counter (never
  in-kernel instrumentation) against the exact
  `n_active·(n_active−1)/2 + n_active·(n−n_active)` formula, strictly
  below all-pairs `n·(n−1)/2`
- Worker `Config` gains `n_active` (0-sentinel = all-active, byte-identical
  to the M0.4 seed) + a `TestParticleType` enum with a single value
  `FeelOnly=0` — a semiactive variant is unrepresentable in the typed API
  and a raw request throws loudly at the ctor; seed-consistency validated
  (slot < n_active must be gravitating, slot ≥ n_active must be
  non-gravitating + massless)
- `detect_regime` / dominant-mass scan / `system_energy` narrowed to
  `.first(n_active_)` so a test particle can never trigger an encounter,
  define the dominant mass, or enter the conserved-energy diagnostic; the
  snapshot's `FrameMeta.count` stays at the total (render still draws probes)
- Confirmed before wiring: Phase-24's `wh_step` already advances every
  `states_` slot, so massless test particles Kepler about the dominant mass
  for free — zero new integration code; a live bit-identity lock: active
  trajectory bit-identical over K steps with vs without test particles in
  the real loop

**Key decisions:** contiguous `[0,n_active)`/`[n_active,n)` partition over a
scattered per-body flag — only a contiguous cut keeps the active block a
textual superset-free copy of the existing kernel, which is what makes the
byte-identity structural rather than incidental; a semiactive variant
deferred as a pure forward-compatible enhancement (zero back-reaction ⇒
zero compounding risk), named blocker = its own summation-order lock plus
review, re-add trigger = craft-tier gravity.

**Deviations:** none — both waves landed as planned; the Phase-24 drift
coverage confirmed true on read, no extra drift call needed. Post-phase:
Phase 27 benchmarked the O(n_active·N) visit count against the production
kernel directly; M1.1's craft-tier warp-session loops reuse the same
"bound the loop to the set that matters" discipline.

**Deep-dive:** posted in #technical-devlog

## Phase 27: Performance + Benchmark — ✅ 2026-06-15

**Goal:** honestly measure whether the Wisdom-Holman base (Phase 24) + the
test-particle tier (Phase 26) are faster than the M0.4 Yoshida4
baseline, carrying forward the discipline that every benchmark asserts the
real path dispatched, cross-checked against a deterministic force-eval
count.

**What it delivered:**
- First-pass cost model + Pareto sweep over the shared frozen 5-body SSB
  ICs + matched horizon — the honest comparison surfaced a correctness
  bug, not a perf answer: WH's `|dE/E|` floored at ~2.7e-5, step-independent
  and growing secularly with horizon, against Yoshida4's flat ~1e-13 —
  impossible for a correct symplectic map. A follow-up run confirmed
  Phase 26's O(n_active·N) visit count against the production kernel
  directly.
- Production seed reverted to Yoshida4 @ 300 s (a barycenter-drift probe
  confirmed the Sun frozen — Δr=Δv=0 while the COM drifted ~1.7e9 m); the
  three measurement commits were kept as honest, deterministic-kernel-clean
  evidence;
  Phase 27.5 opened to fix the transform.
- After Phase 27.5's fix, the small-N Pareto still didn't favor WH — so
  Phase 27 resumed with a sharper question: a test swept step size as a
  fraction of the shortest orbital period on a clean COM-frame fixture and
  pinned the real crossover — **dt/T ≈ 0.03–0.05**: below it Yoshida4 wins
  (more accurate AND cheaper), above it Yoshida4 is unusable (400%+ error
  at dt/T=0.5) while WH stays bounded ≤~1e-6 even at a half-period step
- A per-step-cost N-sweep found WH's ns/step exceeds Yoshida4's for every N
  from 5 to 320 (ratio 0.26→0.60, never reaching 1) — the per-step cost
  crossover sits at N≈10,000+, confirming WH is structurally not a
  small-N force-loop win
- The warp crossover was gated in default CI; the two-column cost model
  (Yoshida4: 6 force passes/0 Kepler solves; WH: 2 kick passes/N−1 Kepler
  solves/1 jump), both crossover tables, and the full Pareto null result
  were recorded in the milestone's benchmark record, every figure pinned to
  its hardware/step/accuracy target

**Key decisions:** revert the seed, keep the benchmark evidence — the honest
comparison that found the bug stays in the tree rather than being softened;
re-shape the measurement around WH's real lever (step size) instead of
re-running the same small-N comparison after the fix landed; structural
facts (dispatch, counts, crossover direction) gate, wall-clock never does
(hardware-variable).

**Deviations:** the original cost-model-plus-Pareto plan surfaced a
correctness bug rather than a perf result — Phase 27.5 was inserted to fix
it before Phase 27 could close; the final characterization (dt/T crossover)
is a different measurement shape than originally planned (a straight
WH-vs-Yoshida4 Pareto), adopted because the null result on the original
shape demanded a sharper question, not a re-run.

**Deep-dive:** posted in #technical-devlog

## Phase 27.5: WH Frozen-Dominant Fix — ✅ 2026-06-15 (INSERTED)

**Goal:** fix the WH frame-transform bug Phase 27's honest Pareto benchmark
surfaced — the dominant body was frozen (its barycentric position/velocity
never updated across a `wh_step`), dropping its reflex motion and letting the
system barycenter drift, a step-independent secular energy error. Inserted
same-day between Phase 27's two measurement halves.

**What it delivered:**
- Fix 1: `to_democratic_heliocentric` stashes the mass-weighted barycenter
  `R_com` and total momentum `P_total` in the dominant slot instead of its
  raw state; `from_democratic_heliocentric` rebuilds the now-moving
  dominant from them. Round-trip relaxed to a few ULP on the dominant slot
  (reconstruction round-off, sanctioned); the Brouwer energy bound was
  hardened to eccentric/inclined COM-frame fixtures, verified to fail on
  the old code and pass after; COM + momentum locked to roundoff over
  ~634 yr. Production floor: 2.67e-5 → 2.0e-5, secular signature gone
- Stop-and-escalate: WH still didn't beat Yoshida4 at matched accuracy —
  per contract, production stayed on Yoshida4 rather than re-flip to a
  still-losing "fixed" WH. A discarded diagnostic probe found the residual
  scales with the system's FRAME velocity (a +30 km/s boost made a clean
  two-body ~170× worse), not the already-fixed COM position
- Fix 2: root cause is that the Kepler drift is only energy-conserving in
  a zero-net-momentum frame (the WHFast precondition), and the shipped
  5-body table carries the omitted planets' net momentum. `wh_step` now
  subtracts the conserved `v_com` from every velocity before the transform
  (COM rest frame) and restores the bulk `v_com·dt` translation after.
  Production floor: 2.0e-5 → 4.4e-8 (~455×), boost-invariant (a +30 km/s
  case: 1.4e-4 → 8.1e-12); `v_com = 0` exactly for a massless test particle,
  preserving the two-body-equals-`kepler_step` bit-exact reduction
- The flat-floor Pareto gate (which had encoded the original bug as a pass)
  was replaced with an honest correct-integrator gate; WH golden vectors
  were regenerated twice (once per fix) against the corrected map —
  sanctioned, since the golden table pins reproducibility, not physics
  correctness
- Even after both fixes, WH still does not beat Yoshida4 at matched accuracy
  on the small-N production fixture — the stop-and-escalate condition held
  a second time; Phase 27 closes with that finding

**Key decisions:** fix the invariant (barycenter/momentum), not the visible
symptom (frozen Sun) — the residual after Fix 1 showed a deeper precondition
(zero net momentum) that position-correction alone could never satisfy
(energy is Galilean-position-invariant); honor the stop-and-escalate
contract twice rather than declare a partial fix a win.

**Deviations:** the fix required two rounds instead of one — the COM-anchor
transform fix (Fix 1) was necessary but insufficient; the COM-frame velocity
wrap (Fix 2) was the residual's real lever, found only because the
stop-and-escalate contract forced continued investigation instead of
accepting Fix 1's improvement as done.

**Goal:** close the cross-platform determinism contract, holistically
re-verify same-binary determinism over the full integrated WH+Kepler+trigger+
test-particle path, and land the two remaining conserved-quantity
math-locks — energy + angular momentum through a real WH↔IAS15 window
and angular-momentum-deviation + barycenter/momentum drift in regular WH
runs — before the Phase 29 review pass.

**What it delivered:**
- Worker-driven flyby geometry re-calibrated at `step_dt=60 s` genuinely
  switches WH(REGULAR)→IAS15(ENCOUNTER)→WH(REGULAR); max|dE/E| measured
  1.04e-8 → locked 1e-7, max|dL/L| measured ~7e-13 → locked 1e-11, bounded
  random walk (decay fraction ≈0.52, no secular slope) confirmed against an
  in-binary IAS15-tiny-step reference
- A 3-body Jupiter+Saturn-mass eccentric/inclined run, 200k steps
  (~634 yr): instantaneous angular-momentum deviation oscillates ~3.1%
  (physical secular e/i exchange, scales down with perturbation strength) —
  the biased-transform sentinel (log-log slope→1) measured ~-0.03, locked
  <0.5; barycenter/momentum drift at 3e-17, confirming the Phase 27.5
  back-transform fix under eccentric/inclined ICs
- Full path (WH regular + Kepler drift + real predictive changeover + 4
  test particles, 7 slots total) bit-identical same-binary (`==`); a TSan
  fix parked both workers before driving (test-only diagnostic global
  race, not production)
- A frozen full-state golden table (single-level WH-regular, WH↔IAS15
  hybrid) generalizing the earlier golden suites — byte-exact same-family

- The arm64 tier-2 CI lane was NOT added — a named deferral (private repo
  and free-plan org, no paid larger-runner tier); the tier-1 golden

- Holistic verification: Debug 385/385, Release 387/387, TSan 3/3 — all
  M0.2–M0.4 + Phase 23–27.5 locks green together with the 4 new locks;

  `nbody_force.cpp` byte-unchanged

**Key decisions:** consolidation = holistic green-together verification, not a
