## Phase 44: J2 Zonal-Harmonic Oblateness — ✅ 2026-07-12

**Goal:** give oblate primaries a J2 zonal-harmonic perturbation
acceleration, additive on the locked Newtonian kernel, entering only
through the existing perturbation/force paths (WH flat kick, HJS nested
kick, IAS15 force evaluation), off-path byte-identical, with a math-lock
property suite — proving the perturbation-plumbing pattern Phase 45 (1PN)
reuses.

**What it delivered:**
- `OblatenessProps`/`OblatenessTable` — a struct-of-rows table
  slot-parallel to `BodyTable`, zero edits to the locked `nbody_force.hpp`
  types; `has_j2` is an explicit presence flag, so a point-mass body never
  enters the J2 loop and byte-identity is a property of control flow, not
  of a `j2=0.0` sentinel evaluating to zero
- `j2_pair_accel` — the single canonical out-of-line J2 pair-acceleration
  kernel, in the ŝ-projection form equivalent to REBOUNDx's rotated-triad
  `gravitational_harmonics.c`; `+ − × ÷` plus one `det_sqrt`, no libm
- A 9-row authoritative catalog (Sun + Mercury..Neptune), every constant
  verified from a primary source this session with citation + epoch
  comments; two named pairing pitfalls guarded in-comment (Saturn/
  Neptune's non-IAU reference radii; the `J2 = -√5·C̄20` normalization for
  Mercury/Venus/Mars)
- `wh_step` 8-arg oblateness overload — J2 enters via `oblateness_kick` at
  both half-kick sites; the dominant slot participates as both J2 source
  and target (solar J2 is the catalog's dominant physical effect and is
  not swallowed by the Newtonian dominant-skip), while only non-dominant
  slots get an explicit velocity kick (the dominant's reaction is carried
  by the existing COM reconstruction)
- HJS `interaction_kick_masked` Step-2b — J2 added into `a_bary` after the
  locked Newtonian fold (inertial-first; applying J2 directly on Jacobi
  vectors yields the wrong precession rate), carried into Jacobi rows by
  the existing mat fold, each pair gated by the same edge mask as its
  Newtonian pair (the same partition invariant Phase 38 established)
- A math-lock: bit-exact power-of-two scaling and equatorial-mirror
  lanes; axis-permutation/rotation invariance realized as tight
  magnitude-scaled ULP lanes (measured 15/19 ULP, locked 32 — the kernel's
  fixed-slot dot products reassociate under both transforms); 6 z-axis
  hexfloat goldens cross-checked to ≤4 ULP against an in-test libm
  reference; an Earth 700 km/98.19° sun-synchronous secular-rate anchor
  (measured 0.99022°/day vs analytic 0.9856°/day, locked at 3%)
- Full suite green at phase exit: Release **584/584**, Debug **580/580**;
  determinism (`nbody_force.{cpp,hpp}`) diff empty across the whole phase;
  every new translation unit libm-clean (`det_sqrt` only)

**Key decisions:** back-reaction adopted for linear-momentum conservation
(the REBOUNDx pattern) — the earlier "no reaction torque" cut is about
spin, not linear momentum; the warp↔flown handoff corrector's B operator
stays Newtonian-only, a named deferral to the Phase 47 gate rather than a
silent omission; the shared worker force-callback gives Leapfrog/Yoshida4
J2 as well as IAS15 — a uniform force law across every tier handoff,
flagged satisfied-plus for the gate.

**Deviations:** axis-permutation and rotation invariance landed as tight
ULP lanes rather than the plan's bit-exact reading — a faithful
value-invariance realization, not a weakened lock (see deep-dive); no
locked-surface edits, no existing tolerance moved.

## Phase 45: 1PN Post-Newtonian Corrections — ✅ 2026-07-12

**Goal:** add the first-order post-Newtonian (1PN) relativistic
perturbation, additive on the locked Newtonian kernel, entering only
through the perturbation paths Phase 44 established (WH flat kick, HJS
nested kick, worker direct-force callback) — off-path byte-identical,
extending the order-pinning determinism contract.

**What it delivered:**
- The Saha & Tremaine 1994 explicit three-term split of the dominant-mass
  1PN Hamiltonian (user-ratified 2026-07-12 over the `gr_potential` form
  and full EIH): a position-only radial β kick at the existing kick seams,
  a position-only γ drift bracketing the Kepler drift, and a per-orbit
  drift-dt rescale α = dt·(1 − 3μ/(2c²a)) — an exact algebraic
  rearrangement, not an approximation, so it gets both mean motion and
  precession right where the cheaper `gr_potential` class biases mean
  motion
- IAS15/direct tiers use the equivalent Anderson-1975/Schwarzschild
  direct EOM instead, since velocity-dependent forces are native there
- WH flat-tier and HJS nested-tier entries at the pinned order Newtonian
  → J2 → 1PN; HJS's β rides the same self-term site J2 uses for its own
  entry — structurally different from J2's Step-2b placement
- Worker-level PN enablement (default OFF) with pseudo-velocity boundary
  transforms (WH per-step, HJS warp per-session) that keep every
  reader-facing state buffer in TRUE velocities
- The true→pseudo velocity conversion pinned to a fixed 8-iteration sweep
  with no epsilon exit (the same fixed-count idiom the Kepler solver
  established) — REBOUNDx's equivalent data-dependent convergence loop is
  not bit-reproducible
- Sun+Mercury one-orbit Einstein precession recovered to the closed form
  `6πμ/(c²a(1−e²))` at ratio 1.0000 (WH and HJS tiers agree to 5
  significant figures); bounded non-secular PN-Hamiltonian `dE/E` (max
  ~9e-14 WH, <1e-6 HJS); 1/c² Newtonian-recovery scaling; cross-tier
  ST94-vs-direct-EOM agreement <1e-4 relative
- Four byte-exact PN-on determinism goldens (WH flat, HJS single, HJS
  nested, worker direct-EOM) plus the OFF-path byte-identity matrix;
  `nbody_force.cpp` diff empty across the whole phase, the new PN
  translation unit libm-clean but for `det_sqrt`
- Phase exit gate green: Release 620/620, no production flip this phase
  (the ON-flip landed with Phase 46.1)

**Key decisions:** the explicit ST94 split over `gr_potential` (mean-motion
bias rejected) and over full EIH (`O(N²)` cost for `≲1e-3×` gain on a
`1e-8` effect); an explicit-only determinism posture over fixed-count
implicit iteration, ruling out REBOUNDx's epsilon-exit convergence; the
formulation was ratified by the user before planning proceeded
(2026-07-12); 1PN+J2 ship ON in the production seed once Phase 46/46.1
validation locks green, deferred to that phase rather than this one.

**Deviations:** the pseudo-velocity boundary transform's placement (WH
per-step vs. HJS per-session) was left open by the plan for the executor
to resolve; per-session storage for WH would have left `states_` carrying
pseudo-velocities between conversions, contradicting the snapshot/
telemetry/detector/handoff readers that all expect true velocities —
resolved to per-step for WH, per-session at the HJS warp session boundary
instead, with `states_` true at every read site. The HJS nested
determinism golden's first capture went all-NaN from hand-set Jacobi
initial conditions geometrically inconsistent with the tree hierarchy;
fixed by seeding physical barycentric conditions and `b2g`-transforming,
the same trap and fix Phase 44 hit at its own nested seed.

## Phase 46: Perturbation Validation + Math-Lock Consolidation — ✅ 2026-07-12

**Goal:** validate both M0.8 perturbations and consolidate the math-locks —
calibrate-then-lock the Mercury perihelion-precession benchmark
(≈42.98″/century 1PN contribution), quantify DE441 multi-epoch
position-error deltas showing 1PN+J2 ≤ Newtonian-only for inner planets,
prove the perturbation on/off regression matrix (OFF → byte-identical to
pre-M0.8 kernel, ON → physical tolerances, all locks green), and confirm
the M0.7 nested seed runs unregressed with perturbations enabled.

**What it delivered:**
- DE441 data foundation: 5 new GM pins (Mercury/Venus body GMs,
  Saturn/Uranus/Neptune system GMs) with by-construction masses and math-lock
  rows, re-verified against JPL SSD's DE440 table; a 14-body × 4-epoch
  whole-day DE441 ladder fixture fetched live from Horizons, base-epoch
  column byte-verified identical to the M0.7 fixture's own base column
- An isolated Sun+Mercury flat-WH run (~2-day step, 1000 yr) recovers
  **42.9807 ″/cy** (0.0016% from Genova 2018's 42.98), locked 42.98 ± 0.30
  ″/cy; solar-J2 secondary 0.0284019 ″/cy (0.7% from the closed form);
  PN+J2 sum directional; the energy channel is bounded at the round-off
  floor (no secular drift from Phase 45's per-step boundary placement)
- Live seed grown 9 → 14 bodies (Sun + 8 planets + Moon + 4 Galileans) on a
  canonical 13-edge in-order Jacobi ladder, test-side twin fixture landing
  first; perturbations OFF (the ON flip stays owned by Phase 46.1) —
  every OFF-path golden byte-unmoved
- One 14-body seed integrated twice (OFF vs 1PN+J2 ON) against the DE441
  ladder on the shipped nested map — Mercury +50yr 7963.0 → 56.66 km
  (140×), Venus 69×, Earth 14×, Mars 38×; Mars promoted to a hard
  err_ON ≤ err_OFF gate given a measured 38-53× margin; Galilean SSB errors
  collapse too (Ganymede +50yr 2.09e6 → 998 km)
- An on/off matrix across WH/HJS/IAS15 — OFF cells byte `==` against the
  legacy pre-M0.8 call shape on all three force paths (tolerance
  forbidden — it would mask a partially-wired perturbation); every ON cell
  anti-wiring-differs from OFF before its energy ceiling is checked
  (WH 6e-4, HJS 2e-9, IAS15 1.5e-7, all calibrate-then-locked)
- The exact M0.7 9-body nested seed run with perturbations ON — no
  eligibility-gate or warp↔flown handoff regression; Galilean jovicentric
  residuals collapse when Jupiter's J2 turns on (Io 56774 → 85 km, 667×;
  Europa 3137×; Ganymede 1134×; Callisto 1186×)
- Consolidation exit gate: Release 630/630, Debug 626/626, math-lock

  audit confirmed zero deletions in the constants file and byte-untouched

## Phase 46.1: All-Planet Validation Seeds — ✅ 2026-07-12

**Goal:** validate the outer-planet J2 catalog rows no other phase dynamically
exercises — test-side per-system satellite seeds for Saturn (Mimas+Tethys),
Uranus (Miranda+Ariel), and Neptune (Naiad+Proteus) against averaged analytic
J2 secular rates and pinned Horizons ephemerides, with the Uranus seed
asserting the true ~98° spin axis — plus a DE441 multi-epoch barycenter ladder
extended to the full planetary set, and the shipped-config perturbations-ON
flip. Inserted 2026-07-10 (user-locked) between Phase 46 and the Phase 47
review pass.

**What it delivered:**
- A shared test-side J2-secular helper (MIT OCW 16.346 Lec 30 averaged nodal/
  apsidal forms, a pole-frame osculating-element wrapper reusing the engine's
  own `extract_kepler_elements`, and an unwrap+LSQ secular-slope fitter),
  anchored by an Earth sun-synchronous unit test (+0.9856°/day, measured 0.03%
  off); libm-allowed test-side only, explicitly barred from the engine's
  `det_` path
- Three per-system seeds (Sun + planet + 2 moons each), each run through its
  natural HJS nested-clump routing, validated two-pronged: engine-measured J2
  secular rates against analytic anchors recomputed from the engine's own
  catalog constants (Saturn: Mimas node 0.66%/apse 0.52%, Tethys node 0.21%;
  Neptune: Naiad node 0.35%, Proteus node 0.07% — the phase's sharpest
  anchors), plus per-epoch Horizons satellite-position residuals locked at
  ~2× measured
- Uranus's seed falsifies a global-z spin-axis bug three independent ways —
  static `|s_hat.z| = 0.2618` sanity, a 104.9× Miranda node-rate ratio
  (catalog axis vs global-z), and a 0.0035° max Ariel orbit-normal lock — using
  an antipode-safe sign rule since the catalog's IAU pole is the antipode of
  the angular-momentum pole (Miranda measures retrograde, i=175.57°, about it)
- The DE441 ladder grown from Phase 46's inner planets to all nine bodies
  (Sun + 8 planet-system barycenters), J2000/+1/+10/+50yr, run at Yoshida4
  direct-force dt=300s with J2+1PN ON through the flown-tier seams; every
  per-body/per-epoch bound tighter than Phase 46's inner-planet-only ladder;
  the 1PN payoff confirmed against a Newtonian-only control (Mercury
  57.9→2.6 km, Venus 98.7→0.33 km at +1yr). A same-day fix added the Sun's
  momentum-conserving PN back-reaction after calibration caught its absence
  injecting barycenter drift
- The shipped-config ON flip — the phase's one engine-source commit: the five
  remaining catalog rows (Mercury/Venus/Saturn/Uranus/Neptune) activated in
  the live 14-body seed alongside the four Phase-44 rows, and PN enabled in
  the worker config — all nine catalogued oblate primaries now ACTIVE, 1PN ON
  across every tier. Landed only after both Phase 46's exit gate and the
  preceding plans' add-only proof were green; `nbody_force.cpp`
  byte-untouched; post-flip Release 643/643, Debug 639/639, math-lock
  1,759,225 assertions across 180 cases, clean 25s headless smoke

**Key decisions:** test-side satellite seeds only, the live seed's satellites
staying Earth's Moon + the four Galileans; moon selection by J2-signal
strength over marquee names; analytic anchors recomputed from the engine's
own catalog constants rather than independently-typed literals, so the
check is engine-vs-analytic self-consistency and immune to a (J2, R_ref)
renormalization trap found in an alternate Neptune source; Uranus's antipode-
safe sign rule over a hard-coded negative assertion, since the catalog stores
the IAU pole (the antipode of the angular-momentum pole) and a naive sign
check would reject the correct axis; the momentum-conserving 1PN back-reaction
caught and fixed during calibration rather than shipped with a
barycenter-drifting convention.

**Deviations:** two, both within the plans' own stated discretion — no
long-running tag on the Uranus/Neptune per-system suites (both made it
conditional on runtime; the 4-body 100d/366d runs are instant); Saturn's
prong-(b) position bounds locked per-epoch rather than as one worst-case bound
(faithful to the plan's own per-epoch language).

  determinism angles filed zero findings, the locked kernel byte-identical,
