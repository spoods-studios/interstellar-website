## Phase 12: Force Model — ✅ 2026-05-21

**Goal:** two-body inverse-square gravity computed in float64, with
documented gravitational parameters and a SI-locked API surface the Phase 13
integrator calls every substep. 14 decisions were locked, plus 4
research amendments along the way.

**What it delivered:**
- `engine/include/interstellar/physics/constants.hpp` — 7 `inline constexpr double` pins with provenance comments: G (CODATA), R_Earth equatorial/mean (WGS84), g₀ (CGPM 1901), μ_Earth (IAU 2015 nominal), μ_Earth_DE441 (HORIZONS regression only), μ_Sun (JPL DE440/DE441). μ values stored directly, never composed from G·M — G's 2.2e-5 relative uncertainty vs 9–11-digit published GM fits
- `evaluate_force(double mu, Vec3f64 r_relative)` in `force.hpp` / `physics_force.cpp` — returns acceleration in m/s² (mass folded into μ), input float64 relative meters (subtract-before-convert preserved, no int64 in the kernel), r³ computed as `r2 * sqrt(r2)` (never `pow(r,3)`), Debug-only `r² > 0` assert with Release IEEE-754 Inf propagation into Phase 13's `try_to_absolute` filter, no softening/saturation
- Forward-scaffolds for M0.4: `force_fn` function-pointer alias (not `std::function` — ~5 vs 6+ cycles/call plus construction cost) and the 8-line `evaluate_pair_forces` Newton's-third-law wrapper
- `test_constants.cpp` — bit-exact equality pins against source literals (catches transcription typos), plus the Earth-surface closed-form `μ/R² ≈ 9.79828 m/s²` check with the g₀-distinction comment
- `test_force.cpp` — 5 math-lock cases (closed-form g, inverse-square scaling, bit-exact odd symmetry, Earth/Sun mass-ratio, r³ refactor lock proving `r3_ref != r3_pow` before pinning the kernel bit-exactly) + a non-asserting ULP-gap benchmark
- CMake wiring creating `tests/unit/integrator/`; M0.2 math-lock 73/73 green Debug + Release, suite at 80

**Key decisions:** μ-only kernel API (acceleration out, not force — no
mass division at the substep boundary); bit-exact constant pins over
ULP-bounded; closed-form 9.79828 m/s² as the surface-g target, not
g₀ = 9.80665; `r² · sqrt(r²)` locked by a dedicated refactor-lock test;
Release r²==0 handled by Inf propagation, zero hot-path branches; no
Plummer softening — biases energy; representative-point tests now,
property suites in Phase 16.

**Deviations:** none in-phase — 6/6 tasks as planned, one
`.gitignore` line (`build-*/`) folded in as infrastructure. Post-phase
(2026-06-04): a finding corrected `MU_EARTH_DE441` from the
DE430/431-era value to the true DE440/441 `3.98600435507e14` and
extended `MU_SUN` to full DE441 precision, pin tests updated with
reviewer sign-off; M0.4's `nbody_force.cpp` (later the locked
deterministic-force kernel) took over production force with an N=1
bit-identity gate routed through the byte-identical `evaluate_force`
expression. `physics_force.cpp`, `force.hpp`, and `test_force.cpp` are
unmodified since 2026-05-21.

## Phase 13: Integrator Core — ✅ 2026-05-21

**Goal:** single-threaded Leapfrog (KDK) and Yoshida 4th-order
integrators sharing one step interface, with a per-substep
`try_to_absolute` NaN/Inf filter that closes a previously identified
NaN-propagation gap structurally. Caller picks the method at
construction; step size fixed and configurable; simulation clock is
integrator-driven, not wall-clock-driven. 22 decisions were locked,
with research amendments adding a concept constraint and expanding one
coefficient identity into six.

**What it delivered:**
- `engine/include/interstellar/physics/integrator.hpp` — POD `State { Vec3f64 r, v; }`, tag structs `Leapfrog`/`Yoshida4`, C++20 `IntegratorMethod` concept, `bound_force_fn` function-pointer alias (μ pre-bound; ~5-cycle call vs `std::function` heap + construction), Yoshida `w₁ = 1/(2−2^(1/3))`, `w₀ = −2^(1/3)/(2−2^(1/3))` as `inline constexpr double` to 25 sig figs (cbrt not constexpr in C++20), and the single `step<M>(span<State>, dt, f, origin)` declaration — span-of-1 in M0.3, span-of-N in M0.4 without touching the kernel
- `physics_integrator_leapfrog.cpp` + internal `physics_integrator_detail.hpp` — shared KDK substep primitive (`v += a·dt/2; x += v·dt; v += a·dt/2`) with `check_finite_or_throw` at all three substep boundaries: NaN/Inf → `std::runtime_error` naming method + substep; float64 state never quantized between substeps (the conversion is called for its validation contract only)
- `physics_integrator_yoshida.cpp` — Form A composition, three KDK substeps at widths (w₁, w₀, w₁); the middle substep runs backward ~1.7·dt (no positive-coefficient 4th-order composition of S₂ exists); 6 force evaluations per step
- `test_yoshida_coefficients.cpp` — identities Y-1..Y-6; Y-3 (`2·w₁³ + w₀³ = 0`, the BCH 4th-order condition) is the load-bearing pin: a sign-flipped w₀ yields ≈9.86, and Y-1's sum jumps to ≈4.05
- `test_leapfrog_step.cpp` — bit-exact single KDK step against a hand-derived expected (pins the operator order against a stealth KDK→DKD refactor that would pass the energy gate) + one-period closure at 1e-3
- `test_yoshida_step.cpp` — one-period closure at 1e-10 (7 orders past the Leapfrog bar at the same dt — the dt⁴ vs dt² gap) + dt⁴ convergence ratio, band [8, 32] per halving
- `test_nan_propagation.cpp`: 5 injection cases throw upstream AND M0.2's `g_to_absolute_release_violation_logged` atomic_flag stays un-set, Debug and Release both
- `sim_clock.hpp` + `test_sim_clock.cpp` — header-only `SimClock` with `const double step_dt_` (immutability at the type level; symplecticity rests on fixed-step composition), non-copy/non-move, own header so worker + HUD skip integrator includes
- Suite 80 → 97, green Debug + Release

**Key decisions:** free-function template `step<M>` over v-table
dispatch, variant visit hoisted to the Phase 14 worker boundary; KDK
form so (x, v) synchronize at step boundaries and energy probes see no
½dt phase bias; Form A composition — 2 constants instead of the
7-coefficient expanded table, ~2 redundant FMA per step as the price
of kernel reuse; six coefficient identities instead of one; throw as
the NaN contract — worker catches and hard-stops; the filter exercises
`try_to_absolute` per substep without quantizing; fixed dt as a
structural contract — adaptive stepping breaks the
modified-Hamiltonian guarantee.

**Deviations:** none in-phase — all planned files landed as specified.
Post-phase: the M0.3 gate (2026-06-04) extended the substep filter to
velocity (a non-finite v from the final half-kick had escaped the
position-only check) and swapped SimClock to integer-count time
derivation — the exact change reserved earlier; M0.4 Phase 19 widened
the seam to a whole-vector `bound_accel_fn`; M0.5 wired Wisdom-Holman
into the worker variant; the M0.6 gate split `KdkMethod` from
`IntegratorMethod` so `step<M>` admits only Leapfrog/Yoshida4. The KDK
primitive remains the base of both fixed-step tiers today.

## Phase 14: Threading + Coordinate Integration — ✅ 2026-05-21

**Goal:** promote the Phase 13 integrator into a dedicated physics worker
thread reading the origin via `CoordinateService::get_origin()` while the
main thread `set_origin`s between frames — the first real concurrent
reader of the M0.2 seqlock, closing a known concurrency gap with an
empirically stressed contention test. 24 decisions were locked, plus 4
research amendments.

**What it delivered:**
- `worker_thread.hpp` / `physics_worker_thread.cpp` — `PhysicsWorker` owning a `std::jthread` with `stop_token` cooperative shutdown; fixed-timestep accumulator (`accumulator_ += time_scale * wall_dt`, integer `step_dt` drains, fractional carry-over) with the 0.25 s Fiedler spiral-of-death clamp; time-scale clamped [1/1024, 1024]; member declaration order = shutdown order (`coord_service_` first, `thread_` last)
- Trivially-copyable `Snapshot` POD (integrator state, sim_time, origin, period/energy fields for Phase 15) published via double buffer — release-store on `index_`, acquire-load in `latest_snapshot()`; control atomics (`paused_`, `time_scale_`) relaxed
- Asymmetric pause primitive: hot loop busy-steps (no sleeps — the integrator is the work); paused branch blocks on `condition_variable_any::wait` with the C++20 `stop_token` overload (P0660R10), one primitive for both resume and shutdown
- An exception path: worker body in `try/catch(...)` capturing `std::exception_ptr` under `error_mutex_`; main's `check_and_rethrow()` surfaces Phase 13 NaN-substep throws on the main thread instead of `std::terminate`
- A synthetic-dt hook `tick(double)`: deterministic accumulator drive for tests; two workers fed identical tick sequences produce bit-identical `State` (exact-FP `step_dt = 1/256`, zero-tolerance REQUIREs) — the reproducibility anchor for later validation work
- SDL keybinds in main.cpp: Space pause-toggle, Comma ×0.5, Period ×2.0, all `!event.key.repeat`-guarded; worker constructed after `VulkanContext` so reverse-of-declaration RAII joins the jthread first
- `test_seqlock_contention.cpp` closing that gap: ~60 Hz monotonic writer + kHz reader + production worker against one service; zero torn reads, per-axis monotonicity, seq-bump audit inferred as 2 × write count (no accessor added), 60.01 s closure run passed on Fedora dev; duration via `INTERSTELLAR_SEQLOCK_CONTENTION_SECONDS` (5 s CI default — the ~5–20 ns race window makes short runs regression detectors only, ~1 window hit per 800 s)
- `tsan_suppressions.txt` scoped to the seqlock's intentional retry-bounded race, injected via a separate `add_test` `ENVIRONMENT` so other tests run TSan unfiltered; suite 97 → 110, M0.2 source byte-identical

**Key decisions:** `std::jthread` over raw thread + atomic flag (RAII
join, stop_token threads through the CV wait); double buffer over
triple/seqlock for the snapshot — sufficient for one publish per read,
later falsified (see Deviations); worker never calls `set_origin` or
SDL; seq-bump audit inferred rather than adding a `seq_for_test()`
accessor; `tick()` bypasses `paused_` — the plan's paused-no-op tick
contradicted its own pause-then-drive test setup, so the pause gate
lives in the hot loop only.

**Deviations:** the TSan run was blocked on 2026-05-21 — no libtsan
runtime on the Fedora host — and logged as a named blocker. Closing it
on 2026-06-04 caught two real races the 60 s contention test missed:
the double buffer failed under two publishes per read (replaced with
a single-`Snapshot` seqlock mirroring the M0.2 origin pattern) and the
tests' `sleep_for(20ms)` pause handshake was not a happens-before edge
(replaced with a `parked_` release/acquire ack, `wait_until_parked()`).
Post-phase: the M0.3 gate added Config guards, an idle sleep, and
wired `rel_energy_error`; a later fix resolved a pause lost-wakeup by
storing `paused_` under `pause_mutex_`; M0.4 Phase 18 replaced the
scalar snapshot with the SoA triple-buffer container.

## Phase 14.5: Swapchain Acquire Fix — ✅ 2026-05-23 (INSERTED)

**Goal:** eliminate the unhandled `vk::OutOfDateKHRError` throw on
swap-chain acquire after compositor activity (KDE workspace switch,
minimize/restore, fullscreen toggle) — M0.1 Phase 3 swap-chain code
surfaced by Phase 15's visual UAT, inserted between 14 and 15 following
the Phase 10.5 precedent. Retroactive: the fix landed first, and the
phase was inserted around it 22 minutes later.

**What it delivered:**
- Both `acquireNextImage` call sites in `engine/src/vulkan_context.cpp`
  (`render_frame`, then-line 882; `render_orbit_frame`, then-1268) wrapped
  in try/catch — `vk::OutOfDateKHRError` → `recreate_swap_chain()` + early
  return, mirroring the present-side pattern in place at then-`:924,1304`.
  The prior `if (acquire_result == vk::Result::eErrorOutOfDateKHR)`
  branches were dead code: vk-hpp's throwing overload raises the exception
  because `eErrorOutOfDateKHR` is not in `acquireNextImage`'s success-code
  list, so the throw propagated past the check to `main()`
- `eSuboptimalKHR` preserved as a Result check inside the try — it IS a
  success code, still arrives as a value, and flips `resize_requested_`
  for after-present recreation instead of dropping the frame
- Early return before `resetFences` kept: the fence is not signaled by a
  submit on failed acquire; resetting would deadlock the next
  `waitForFences`
- Five-line call-site comment stating the throwing-overload rule, added
  the same evening
- Net diff `vulkan_context.cpp` only, +27/−18; 115/115 ctest green Release
  (M0.2 math-lock unbroken); empirical UAT re-run of the crashing scenario
  logged clean `Swap chain recreated: 2552x1556` cycles, zero terminations

**Key decisions:** mirror the present-side try/catch (same overload
semantics, surviving since Phase 10) rather than a new shape;
dual-detection contract from the 2026-04-09 decision now implemented
on the acquire side; scope split — the visual UAT's remaining items
stayed in Phase 15's fix-loop as new-code bugs fixed in-phase, the
M0.1-era shader deficiencies became Phase 15.5, only this acquire fix
closed retroactively same-day.

**Deviations:** none — single-commit phase, fix verified before
insertion. Post-phase: the try/catch blocks are byte-identical as of
2026-07-21 (handlers today at `:920/:962/:1310/:1349` after unrelated
edits shifted lines); no third acquire site added; Phase 15.5's
decision cites this phase by name as the provenance pattern.

## Phase 15: Validation — ✅ 2026-06-04

**Goal:** prove the integrated Phase 12–14 stack produces scientifically
accurate two-body orbits — automated math-lock asserts plus a user-run
visual UAT closing the M0.3 validation contract. 24 decisions were
locked, plus one mid-phase amendment.

**What it delivered:**
- `test_energy_conservation.cpp`: relative energy drift |ΔE/E| on the canonical 7000 km circular Earth orbit (dt = 10 s, 10⁵ steps, KDK-boundary sampling of ε = v²/2 − μ/r), calibrated floors Leapfrog 3.4e-9 / Yoshida4 1.1e-13, locked thresholds 1e-6 / 1e-9; a second assert requires `max_yoshida × 100 ≤ max_leapfrog` (measured ratio 31196) to catch a mis-composed Yoshida degrading to second order
- `physics/kepler.hpp` + `physics_kepler.cpp` — Curtis/Vallado state → classical-elements extractor ({a, e, i, Ω, ω, ν} + period, plane normal, periapsis direction, |h|), singular cases return documented conventions, never throws; feeds both the invariants test below and the demo's predicted conic
- `test_kepler_invariants.cpp`: a, e, |L| bounded over 100 periods at ~3× measured floors: 1e-12 relative (a), 5e-8 absolute (e — the eccentricity vector `(v×h)/μ − r̂` subtracts near-unit quantities on a circular orbit), 1e-12 relative (|L|)
- `test_horizons_sanity.cpp`: Sun-Earth two-body vs JPL HORIZONS DE441 at +1d/+1w/+1mo/+1yr, bounds at measured floor × 2 (250 / 10,000 / 65,000 / 1.3e6 km) with the missing-physics budget documented: 112 km at +1d is the unmodeled Earth-Moon barycenter wobble (4671 km amplitude, theory match ~10%), not integrator error
- `test_long_horizon.cpp` (opt-in `[.long]`): 1000 periods via the worker's synthetic-dt `tick()` (time_scale 1024, exact power-of-2 wall_dt), same invariant bounds as the Kepler-invariants test above
- Orbit demo: SDF-disk Earth/Moon, 2000-slot fading trail, per-frame predicted conic from live Kepler elements, font8x8 HUD (T+, period, live |ΔE/E|), three new pipelines on M0.2's push-constant plumbing, J2000 epoch from `horizons_earth_moon_j2000.hpp`

**Key decisions:** calibrate-then-lock — measure floors first, lock
at 2–3×; per-method tiered energy thresholds (a single 1e-9 gate would
false-positive a correct second-order Leapfrog); the HORIZONS sanity
test was redirected mid-phase from geocentric Moon to Sun-Earth —
solar tide on the Moon is 1.3% of Earth's pull, 28,000 km/day drift,
not a two-body problem; its bounds are loose by ephemeris standards
but tight enough to catch sign flips, dt typos, wrong μ, and IC swaps;
long-horizon must not slacken short-horizon bounds.

**Deviations:** the Sun-Earth redirect (research drift estimate off
by 4–5 orders of magnitude) and the demo fix-loop of 2026-05-23: trail
oversampling into sub-pixel duplicates, per-frame HUD buffer clobber,
and `Snapshot.rel_energy_error` never written — all new-code bugs
fixed in-phase. The visual UAT also surfaced two M0.1-era debts fixed
in their own phases: the swapchain acquire throw (Phase 14.5) and the
aspect/projection issue (Phase 15.5). The visual UAT passed
2026-06-04 at 118/118 tests. Post-phase: the M0.3 gate strengthened
the locks (full extractor surface, Moon-IC energy lock at e ≈ 0.69,
every-boundary |L|); M0.4's three-body case tightened the HORIZONS
bounds ~20×; the energy thresholds, invariant bounds, and HORIZONS
distances are unchanged since 2026-05-21.

## Phase 15.5: Render Projection Hygiene — ✅ 2026-06-04 (INSERTED)

**Goal:** close two M0.1-era projection defects surfaced by Phase 15's
visual UAT (2026-05-23): no aspect-ratio compensation in any
orbit-demo shader (a 2552×1556 window stretches the near-circular Moon

decision — the third debt item closed in its own dedicated phase,
after Phase 10.5 and Phase 14.5. 5 decisions were locked for this fix.

**What it delivered:**
- `engine/include/interstellar/render/projection.hpp` — header-only
  `project_to_ndc(world_xy, ndc_scale, aspect)` = `{x·scale/aspect, y·scale}`,
  the CPU source of truth; GLSL PARITY comment pins the byte-equivalent
  `gl_Position` expression `line.vert`/`disk.vert` must compute
- `tests/unit/render/test_projection.cpp` — 3 Catch2 contract cases
  tagged `[render][projection]` in source (plus an internal tracking
  tag): origin → NDC (0,0) exact; aspect divides x / preserves y (a
  circle stays a circle); aspect = 1 no-op
- `aspect` push constant by renaming spare `_pad0`@offset 36 in all four
  48-byte `ShapePushConstants` mirrors — rename only, every
  `static_assert(sizeof == 48)` holds, no pipeline-layout change
- `disk.vert` + `line.vert`: `gl_Position.x = world_xy.x * pc.ndc_scale /
  pc.aspect` (y untouched); `line.vert`'s non-canonical push block rewritten
  to the shared shape with `camera_relative_center` subtraction — a no-op
  for the (0,0,0) Earth-origin demo; `hud.vert` keeps layout parity via

- Wiring: `OrbitDemo::render()` computes `aspect = width/height` once per
  frame, sets it at the conic + trail push writes and both
  `DiskRenderer::draw` calls; `world_to_ndc_scale_` stays isotropic — aspect

- Visual UAT re-run passed (user-verified 2026-06-04): centered,
  aspect-stable under mid-run resize, closed conic tracking the
  trail; 118/118 ctest green Debug + Release, M0.2 + M0.3 math-locks

camera-center hypothesis for the "bottom-right orbit" did not
reconcile (trail/conic are Earth-origin-relative; origin maps to NDC
(0,0)); research attributed the defect to aspect stretch +

term; no true-scale body rendering — trajectory made honest, not body
sizes. Honest UAT criterion: centered + aspect-stable + closed conic,
NOT a literal circle (foreshortening is correct-by-design; a circle
needs a view matrix, out of scope).

projection` matches nothing (Catch2 registers by TEST_CASE name — the
targeted filter uses the internal tracking tag instead). Post-phase:

viewport skip and collapsed the four struct mirrors into a shared
`shape_push_constants.hpp`; Phase 41's camera recenters CPU-side — the
`camera_relative_center` plumbing on line draws is still (0,0,0) as of
2026-07-21. Shaders and test unmodified since this phase.

suite's own orbit generator — perifocal velocity built by

and retrograde via `v.y`-flip alone (collapses L_z 7.0e10 → −6e8;
fixed to full x-axis reflection). Four calibration deviations
surfaced and logged, not silently widened: dt re-centered T/10 →
T/1000, energy/LRL e-grid capped at 0.5, Leapfrog bounds locked
1–2.5 OOM over theory, dt-sweep band re-centered to [T/2000, T/500].

the same-day M0.3 gate strengthened the lock (every-step
angular-momentum excursions, a Moon-IC e≈0.69 grid-gap pin, corrected

recording `yoshida_w1` 1 ULP below the cbrt form); a later fix
corrected the `[.long]` gating docs (the aggregate runs in default
ctest, ~1 s). Calibrated bounds untouched since 2026-06-04; the suite
stayed green through the M0.8 gate (2026-07-13).
